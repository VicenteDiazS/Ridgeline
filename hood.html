Front-bay quick reference for your VIN-decoded 2019 Ridgeline 2WD. The V6 is a 3.5-liter SOHC i-VTEC engine, the primary under-hood fuse box is on the passenger-side damper-house area, and the interior jump-start negative point is not the buried negative battery post.

Engine Quick Info

Name
3.5-liter, 24-valve SOHC i-VTEC V-6
Fueling
Direct injection
Output
280 hp at 6,000 rpm
Torque
262 lb-ft at 4,700 rpm
Drive
2WD from VIN 5FPYK2F64KB002267

Fuse Boxes

  • Fuse Box A: passenger-side damper-house area under the hood.
  • Fuse Box B: near the brake-fluid reservoir on the opposite side of the bay.
  • Cabin fuse box: under the driver-side lower dash.

What forum posts usually call these: Fuse Box B often gets described as the brake-fluid-side box or the driver-side rear under-hood box. Fuse Box A usually gets called the passenger-side under-hood box or the strut-tower / damper-house-side box.

Fuse type note: Honda separates the large screw-down engine-bay fuses from the smaller puller-style fuses. The fuse type column below is an on-page service label based on that distinction: high-current bolt-down for the large engine-bay blocks and blade / puller-style for the smaller service fuses.

Wiring And Electrical

Positive jump connection is at the battery. The commonly cited Ridgeline jump-start ground point is the engine hanger bracket under the engine cover, not the hidden negative terminal.

Battery 12V Group 48 / H6 fitment
Common CCA ~680 to 760 A
Check First Installed label on your current battery

Parts And Service

  • Battery fitment footprint: BCI Group 48 / DIN H6.
  • Battery size: 12 1/16 x 6 7/8 x 7 9/16 in.
  • Replacement chemistry can be flooded, EFB, or AGM in the same case size.
  • Write your actual installed battery brand, CCA, and install date here after you confirm it on the label.

Battery Fitment Diagram

This is the case-size footprint most replacement guides list for the 2019 Ridgeline. CCA is not fixed by the case size, so always confirm the label on the battery currently installed in your truck.

Battery And Jump Notes

  • Use a 12-volt booster battery only.
  • Connect positive at the battery's positive terminal.
  • Use the specified engine-bay ground point for the negative lead.
  • Keep the fuse-box cover diagrams handy before troubleshooting power issues.

Source language behind this section: forum posts often describe the battery as being tucked under the hood with the negative side buried, while the manual-style guidance points to the engine hanger bracket as the jump ground.
