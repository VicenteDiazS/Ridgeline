Maintenance Minder Code Guide

Main Items

CodeMeaning
AReplace engine oil.
BReplace engine oil and oil filter, inspect front and rear brakes, check parking brake adjustment, inspect tie-rod ends, steering gearbox and boots, suspension components, driveshaft boots, brake hoses and lines including ABS/VSA, all fluid levels and condition, exhaust system, and fuel lines and connections.

Honda's 2019 Ridgeline Maintenance Minder sheet also says to replace brake fluid every 3 years regardless of the main-item display and to adjust the valves during services A, B, 1, 2, or 3 if they are noisy.

Sub Items

CodeMeaning
1Rotate tires.
2Replace engine air filter, replace cabin dust/pollen filter, inspect drive belt.
3Replace transmission fluid and transfer fluid. On your 2WD truck, the transfer-fluid part does not apply.
4Replace spark plugs, replace timing belt and inspect water pump, inspect valve clearance.
5Replace engine coolant.
6Replace rear differential fluid. This does not apply to your 2WD truck.
7Brake-fluid replacement reminder. Honda also states brake fluid should be replaced every 3 years even apart from the main-item message.

Flat-Tire Basics

  • Park safely, set the parking brake, and block the opposite wheel if needed.
  • Use the jacking point closest to the tire being changed.
  • Lower the truck before final wheel-nut tightening.
  • Tighten wheel nuts in a star pattern to 94 lb-ft.

TPMS Reminder

  • Check tire pressures monthly when cold.
  • The display pressure can be slightly different from a hand gauge.
  • Inflate to the vehicle placard pressure after repair or seasonal changes.

Shop-Lift Notes

  • The roadside jack uses the closest side jacking point.
  • For shop work, use proper lift points and support stands; never rely on the emergency jack alone.
  • If a wheel or jack point looks damaged, stop and inspect before lifting.

Common Garage Jobs

Oil Change

  • Check the truck is level before final oil reading.
  • Replace the filter and crush washer together.
  • Torque the drain bolt to 29 lb-ft.
  • Reset the Maintenance Minder after confirming the level.

Wheel / Tire Service

  • Verify the tire size on the truck before ordering.
  • Torque wheel nuts to 94 lb-ft in a star pattern.
  • Recheck torque after a short drive if wheels were removed.
  • Confirm tire pressure from the driver-door placard.

Battery Service

  • Photograph the installed battery label before replacement.
  • Clean and protect the terminals before reconnecting.
  • Use the engine-bay ground point for jump procedures.
  • Save the new battery info in the Garage Log when done.

Wipers

  • Inspect blade condition at least every six months.
  • Use the maintenance position/service procedure from the owner manual before changing blades.
  • I still have not promoted an exact blade length here because the public sources conflict; verify before ordering.

Service Rhythm

  • Maintenance Minder is the primary service guide.
  • Monthly checks: engine oil, coolant, washer fluid, brake fluid, battery terminals, exterior lights, and tires.
  • When towing or hauling often, keep a closer eye on fluids, brakes, and tire condition.
