Rear Hitch And Towing

Hitch, towing, trailer wiring, and rear utility references.

Connector quick note: Honda says AWD Ridgelines came with a standard 7-pin connector. Your VIN decodes as a 2WD truck, so verify whether your specific truck has the connector installed or only the hitch / tow-plug provision.

Trailer Connector Picture

This is the common rear trailer connector style associated with Ridgeline 7-way harness setups: a round socket with flat blade contacts.

What It Is Called

7-way blade 7-pin RV-style 7-way flat blade

If your truck is equipped with the Ridgeline 7-way harness, this is the connector type you want to match when buying trailer adapters, testers, or replacement sockets.

2WD VIN 3,500 lb max towing
AWD Factory Note Standard 7-pin connector

Connector Types You Can Use

4-flat trailer plug Use a 7-way to 4-flat adapter for basic running, brake, and turn lights.
5-flat trailer plug Use an adapter when your trailer has an added reverse-lockout or auxiliary lead.
6-pole trailer plug Adapters are available for round 6-way connectors used on some utility trailers.
7-way blade trailer plug Direct match when the trailer uses the same RV-style 7-way socket.

Wiring And Towing Notes

  • Tekonsha's custom-fit Ridgeline harness documentation describes a 7-way blade connector.
  • That harness plugs into a gray 12-pin tow plug at the back wall of the cab.
  • A connector bracket is helpful so the plug is fixed near the hitch instead of hanging loose.
  • Use dielectric grease on trailer electrical connections to reduce corrosion.
