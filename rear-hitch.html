Towing Checklist

Before Hookup

  • Confirm the trailer plug matches your truck or adapter.
  • Check hitch pin, coupler latch, chains, and connector latch.
  • On a 2WD Ridgeline, stay within the 3,500 lb properly equipped rating.

Light Check

  • Test running lights, left turn, right turn, brake lights, and reverse if used.
  • If one side is dead, check the adapter first before pulling apart the trailer.
  • Use dielectric grease on exposed trailer electrical connections.

Road Check

  • Recheck hitch pin, coupler lock, and safety chains after a short drive.
  • Watch tire pressure and brake feel more closely when towing in heat.
  • Premium unleaded is recommended by Honda when towing more than 3,500 lb.

Wiring And Towing Notes

  • Tekonsha's custom-fit Ridgeline harness documentation describes a 7-way blade connector.
  • That harness plugs into a gray 12-pin tow plug at the back wall of the cab.
  • A connector bracket is helpful so the plug is fixed near the hitch instead of hanging loose.
  • Use dielectric grease on trailer electrical connections to reduce corrosion.
